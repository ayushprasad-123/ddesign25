Because there is no planet B.

Life on land is essential to the health of our planet and to the well-being of every community. Forests, grasslands, wetlands, and mountains provide clean air, fertile soil, and countless natural resources that sustain human life. Yet these ecosystems face growing pressures from climate change, deforestation, pollution, and habitat loss. SDG 13: Life on Land calls for urgent action to restore degraded environments, safeguard biodiversity, and promote responsible land use that supports both people and nature.

Why Land Matters

Healthy terrestrial ecosystems are the foundation of all life. They regulate the climate, host most of the world’s biodiversity, and support livelihoods for billions of people. When these landscapes are damaged, we see increased natural disasters, reduced food security, and the loss of species that are vital to ecological balance. Protecting land is not only about conservation—it is about creating a sustainable future where communities and ecosystems thrive together.
